Dr. Phil | Endorsements & Investments & Books

Endorsements & Investments

Aside from investing in his shows and real estate, Dr. Phil has sowed a couple of millions of his fortune in technology.

He is actually the co-founder of the telemedicine app Doctor on Demand.

In 2003, Phil got involved in the weight-loss business. He came up with a line of supplements, energy bars, and shakes.

These weight-loss products were also featured in his show, with his nephew Tony and sisters Brenda and Deana offering testimonials on how well the products worked.

However, after The Federal Trade Commission got interested in Phil’s case and looked into the claims, fearing scrutiny, Phil pulled his products from the market in March 2004.

Later FDA investigated the products and claims they made on air.

Phil was sued by the people who used the products and did not see any changes in their weight in 2005.

The following year McGraw settled the case for $10.5 million.

However, of the total amount, $6 million was paid to accusers in the form of Amway brand vitamins.

Similarly, McGraw has endorsed several products and appeared in TV commercials and print ads.

He was featured in print ads for weight management and nutrition products CAS Nutraceuticals‘ Shape Up! in 2003.

Similarly, in 2004 he appeared in the print ads for American Dairy Farmers‘ “Got Milk?” milk mustache campaign and M&M Candies in 2008.

In 2006, he signed an endorsement deal with Match.com.

Dr. Phil served as an official spokesperson for AstraZeneca, a diabetes drug, in 2016.

Furthermore, McGraw filmed a television commercial for Acura Automobiles in 2012.

Dr. Phil | Career

In 1975, McGraw graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in psychology from Midwestern State University in Wichita Falls, Texas.

In 1976, he got his Master’s and, in 1979, completed his Ph.D. in clinical psychology from the University of North Texas.

McGraw founded CSI Courtroom Sciences, Inc., an organization that advised individual plaintiffs on how to achieve settlements.

Oprah invited Phil to her show in 1998. After his successful appearances, he began appearing weekly on “The Oprah Winfrey Show.”

The early appearances on “The Oprah Winfrey Show” made Phil a household name and, over time, led to his own show.

“The Dr. Phil Show” debuted in 2002 and is still on the air today.
