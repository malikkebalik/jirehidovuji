    Dascha Polanco is a Dominican American actress who has a net worth of $5 million. Dascha Polanco is best known for playing the role of Dayanara Diaz on the Netflix series "Orange Is the New Black." Polanco won an ALMA Award in 2014 and a Screen Actors Guild Award in 2015 for Orange Is the New Black. She has also had notable roles on the programs "The Assassination of Gianni Versace: American Crime Story" and "Russian Doll." Additionally, Polanco has been in several films, including "The Perfect Match," "In the Heights," and the animated comedy "DC League of Super-Pets."

    Early Life and Education

    Dascha Polanco was born on December 3, 1982 in Santo Domingo, Dominican Republic. When she was young, she moved with her family to the Brooklyn borough of New York City, where she was raised by her cosmetologist mother and mechanic father. Polanco has a younger sister and a younger brother. As a teenager, she moved with her family to Miami, Florida. Polanco returned to New York City for her higher education to attend Hunter College, from which she received her bachelor's degree in psychology.

    Career Beginnings

    Following her college graduation, Polanco entered the health care industry with the goal of becoming a nurse. While studying in the field, she worked in administration at Montefiore Medical Center in the Bronx. Eventually, Polanco decided she wanted to pursue an acting career instead, and enrolled herself at BIH Studios. She went on to sign with the talent agency Shirley Grant Management.

    Television Career

    Polanco made her acting debut on television in 2011 in an episode of the CBS police procedural series "Unforgettable." The next year, she appeared in an episode of another CBS police procedural series, "NYC 22." Polanco subsequently had her breakthrough role in 2013 when she began playing Dayanara "Daya" Diaz, a Puerto Rican inmate, on the Netflix series "Orange is the New Black." Following the show's first two seasons, in which she appeared in a recurring capacity, Polanco became part of the main cast for the remaining five seasons through 2019. She was joined in the large ensemble cast by Taylor Schilling, Laura Prepon, Kate Mulgrew, Uzo Aduba, Danielle Brooks, Natasha Lyonne, Nick Sandow, and Taryn Manning, among many others. For her work on "Orange is the New Black," Polanco shared two SAG Awards for Outstanding Performance by an Ensemble in a Comedy Series with her cast mates.
