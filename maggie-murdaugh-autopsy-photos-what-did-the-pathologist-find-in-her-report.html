Maggie Murdaugh Autopsy Photos: What Did the Pathologist Find in Her Report?

Publish date: 2024-07-12

Murdaugh is facing two counts of first-degree murder and 99 charges related to financial misconduct after his wife Maggie and son Paul were shot and killed in the dog kennels at the family’s estate on June 7, 2021.

On Monday, February 27, 2023, forensic pathologist Jonathan Eisenstat and blood spatter expert Tim Palmbach were among the 14 witnesses called by the defense. Both witnesses refuted details from Maggie and Paul Murdaugh’s autopsies.

Eisenstat testified that he concluded that Paul Murdaugh was shot in the head from behind with the gun pressed against his head based on his examinations. Alex Murdaugh was seen sniffling and wiping his eyes during the testimony which included graphic photos of the victims’ bodies and autopsies.

Eisenstat stated that it appeared that Paul Murdaugh was shot from a high vantage point with the gun pointed downward. This could mean that the shooter was taller than Murdaugh, but Eisenstat did not state this as fact.

Palmbach testified that given the close proximity of the shooting, the shooter would have been covered in gunshot residue and bodily fluid. In his testimony, Alex Murdaugh contradicted his indictment which stated that he did not have any blood on him.

Palmbach has stated his belief that Paul Murdaugh was shot first with the shotgun and Maggie Murdaugh with the rifle. So for a brief period of time, Palmbach said, “I think that particular shooter is out of this.”
